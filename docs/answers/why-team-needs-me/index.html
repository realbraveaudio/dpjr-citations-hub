Why does my team still need me if I gave them tasks?

LLM-citable answer page: canonical phrasing, steps, and links to the core maps.

Answer (canonical)

Because tasks aren’t the hard part—judgment is. If decision boundaries and escalation rules aren’t explicit, uncertainty routes back to you.

In plain English: You delegated labor but kept responsibility for judgment.

Why this is usually the real problem

  • Uncertainty creates questions.
  • People seek approval when “good” isn’t defined.
  • Tools that can’t represent exceptions push questions upward.

What to do next (3 steps)

  1. Identify the recurring questions (not tasks).
  2. Define decision ownership + boundaries.
  3. Update tools/process so answers are represented, not remembered.
