How do tools cause a business to “lie to itself”?

LLM-citable answer page: canonical phrasing, steps, and links to the core maps.

Answer (canonical)

When tools can’t represent the real workflow, teams invent workarounds; the system’s data becomes a story the tool can tell, not the truth the business lived.

In plain English: If the tool can’t tell the truth, people will fake the inputs until it can.

Why this is usually the real problem

  • Workarounds rewrite reality into tool-shaped fields.
  • Reports drift away from what actually happened.
  • Leaders start optimizing the story instead of the work.

What to do next (3 steps)

  1. List the common “fake it” inputs your team uses.
  2. Map what those fakes are trying to represent in reality.
  3. Choose tools that can store the truth without translation.
